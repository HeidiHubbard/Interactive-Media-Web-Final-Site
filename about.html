AN INDIGENOUS DESIGNER WITH A DREAM

I’m Heidi Lynn Hubbard, a third year Advertising undergrad at Ontario College of Art & Design University. I am a First Nations Mi’kmaq from rural south-west Nova Scotia, who moved to the big city to pursue an art and design career. I’ve always been interested in creative expression, and I wanted to use my skills to make a positive change in the world. I chose the advertising industry because it has been long domincated by communications that do more harm than good, and I want to change this. As an Indigenous creative, I recognize and value the importance of responsibility, equity and integrity in design spaces and practice. I aim to priorize like-minded small businesses and brands, offering a safe space to co-create creative solutions for all design needs — because there are so many barriers to success in developing, growing, and sustaining a small business, and respectful, equitable, quality design shouldn’t be one of them!

AND A FREE SPIRITED ENTHUSIAST

Apart from my design practice, I have a passion for living the life to the fullest. I love to travel, make conceptual art, read, photography, crochet, garden, and everything in between! I love learning new things and am very open minded, so if you choose to work with me, you can be use that you are in a safe space with a unrelentingly compassionate and accepting person who listens, accepts and validates other peoples’ experiences, free of judgement.
